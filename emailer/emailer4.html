COVID-19's scale, speed and disruptive impact have no parallels in recent history. It is estimated that nearly 90 percent of global businesses have been hit by the crisis. As businesses continue to focus on fast-tracking recovery, CFOs are increasingly finding themselves in the thick of the action. They are playing a key role in stabilizing businesses in the near-term, creating conducive conditions for growth and prioritizing levers for recovery.


How are CFOs planning to achieve these goals? What are their current imperatives? What role will digital play in the mix? To find answers to questions like these, we supported Everest Group to conduct a survey and reach out to 300 CFOs globally across 20 industries with questions about the future of their businesses. The outcome of this exercise – the Global CFO Survey 2020 – is an insights-rich report that will serve as a guidepost for firms as they chart their roadmaps for recovery.


I'd like to briefly mention the key themes that emerged from the survey: there's a definite rise in demand for third-party service provider collaborations, businesses are focused on accelerating digitalization efforts and investing in future-ready business models. If you'd like to read the entire report, please download it from here: Global CFO Survey-2020


I hope that you'll find the report's findings incisive and informative.
